Background

I hold a Ph.D. in Computational Materials Science from the Norwegian University of Life Sciences (NMBU), an M.S. in Theoretical (Quantum) Chemistry from the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), and a Pharm.D. from Shiraz University of Medical Sciences (SUMS). My academic path bridges medical sciences, quantum chemistry, and computational biology.

Research Interests

My primary research focus lies in neuroimmunology, with an emphasis on the role of B cells in Multiple Sclerosis (MS). I employ computational and bioinformatic approaches to investigate the immune mechanisms involved in neurodegenerative diseases, with the goal of identifying biomarkers and therapeutic targets.

Current Projects

  • Computational Profiling of B-cell Repertoires in MS: Analyzing single-cell and bulk sequencing data to characterize B-cell clonal expansion and antigen specificity.
  • Neuroimmune Signatures in CNS Tissue: Integrating transcriptomic data to explore immune cell infiltration patterns in brain lesions.
