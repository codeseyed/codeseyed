Background

I hold a Ph.D. in Computational Materials Science (NMBU), an M.S. in Theoretical (Quantum) Chemistry (NTNU), and a Pharm.D. (SUMS). My training spans medical sciences, quantum chemistry and computational biology.

Research Interests

My work centres on neuro-immunology, with a special focus on how B-cells contribute to Multiple Sclerosis. Using bioinformatic pipelines I explore immune mechanisms, identify biomarkers and prioritise therapeutic targets.

Current Projects

  • Computational profiling of B-cell repertoires in MS: single-cell and bulk sequencing to map clonal expansion and antigen specificity.
  • Neuro-immune signatures in CNS tissue: integrative transcriptomics of immune infiltration in brain lesions.
